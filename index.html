Teenager's Mental Health Is Important!



"A teenager won't have much stress as an adult." That is what people usually said. People always think a teenager's life is carefree and unstressful. However, teenagers and young adult having a higher risk of mental illness than adult. Growing is a hard process for teenagers, they are under the stress of do well in school, get along with family, and make big decisions. Most of these pressures cannot be aviod, and worrying about them is normal. But feeling very sad, hopeless or worthless could be warning signs of a mental health problem. Reasearch showing that


50% of lifetime cases of mental illness begin at the age of 14.



Causes

Bullying



Bullying is not only affecting the victims of bullying but also the bullies. Research shows that both of them will have a high risk for depression. The bullies are at increased risk for substance use, academic problems, and violence to others later in life. The victims of bullying are at increased risk for attempt suicide, antisocial, and autistic. Also, cyberbullying is affecting more teens than actual bullying.




Relationship



Relationships can including friendships and romantic relationships. Good relationships will help teenagers develop independent skills, increase in self-esteem, and better family relationships. On the other hand, Bad relationships will develop bad habits for a teenager. For example, addicted to drugs, nonstop smoking, symptoms of depression and anxiety.




Family



Family relationship will make a huge impact on a teen's mental health. For example, Divorce may increase the risk for mental health problems in children and teens. Also, having bad relationship with family will make impacts on their grades and giving them more pressure.




Social Media



Internet makes our life more convenient and easier. However, some people are using social media to spread false information that will affect teens' thoughts and feelings. When teen spead too much times on social media, it decreased, disrupted, and delayed their sleep time.
